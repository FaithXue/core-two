Part I

I held

it truth,

with him who

who sings To one clear

harp in divers tones, That men may rise

on stepping-stones Of their dead selves to higher things.

But who shall so forecast the years And find in loss a gain to match?

Or reach a hand thro' time to catch The far-off interest of tears?

Let Love clasp Grief lest both be drown'd,

Let darkness keep her

raven gloss: Ah, sweeter to be drunk with loss,

To dance with death, to beat the ground, Than that the victor Hours should scorn

The long result of love, and boast,

`Behold the man that

loved and lost,

with him who

But all he was

is overworn.'
